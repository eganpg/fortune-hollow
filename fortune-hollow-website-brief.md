# Fortune Hollow — Website Content Brief
**Farm Stay · Lampasas County, Texas**

---

## About Fortune Hollow

Fortune Hollow is a working farm stay on approximately 20 acres in Lampasas County, Texas (Hill Country). Guests stay on the property and participate in real daily farm operations alongside the owners. The experience is hands-on, educational, and immersive — not a petting zoo, but an actual working farm where guests get their hands dirty and leave knowing something real.

A winery is located next door and is part of the Saturday evening experience.

---

## Brand Tone & Voice

- **Boutique but honest.** Elevated, warm, and inviting — but never precious or overstated. This is real farm work and the copy doesn't hide that.
- **The tension is the selling point.** Guests will sweat. They'll also sleep better than they have in months, drink wine at sunset, and eat breakfast they helped produce. Lean into both sides of that.
- **Second person, present tense.** Write to the guest directly: *"You'll wake up before the sun..."* not *"Guests will wake up..."*
- **Specific over generic.** Use Nan, Cognac, Fortune Hollow by name. Avoid generic farm-stay clichés.

**Tagline:** *Get your hands dirty. Sleep like you earned it.*

## The Animals

| Name | Animal | Role / Notes |
|------|--------|--------------|
| **Nan** | Jersey dairy cow | Primary milking animal. Milked twice daily at 6 am and 6 pm with 30-min prep. Jersey milk is exceptionally rich and creamy — high butterfat content. |
| **Cognac** | Longhorn steer | Companion animal and guest favorite. Guests can take him for a walk or spend time with him. Very photogenic. |
| Egg-laying hens | Chickens (layers) | Guests collect eggs in the morning. Used in farm breakfasts. |
| Meat birds | Chickens (broilers) | Raised for meat. Guests learn the difference between layer and meat bird management. |

## The Weekend Experience

### Structure
- **Arrive:** Friday evening
- **Depart:** Sunday by noon
- **Ratio:** Approximately 50% hands-on activity, 50% rest/downtime — the work is genuinely hard and the rest is intentional

### Loose Narrative Overview (for website hero/intro section)

This isn't a petting zoo. It's a real working farm in the Texas Hill Country — and for one weekend, it's yours to be part of. You'll wake up before the sun, pull on your boots, and help milk **Nan**, our Jersey dairy cow, at the break of dawn. You'll collect eggs from the hen house, learn how fresh milk goes from cow to glass, and sit down to a proper farm breakfast made from ingredients you helped bring in that morning.

Along the way you'll spend time with **Cognac**, our Longhorn — whether that's taking him for a slow walk around the property or simply standing next to a set of horns that wide and realizing some things have to be seen in person. The afternoons belong to you: the pool, the shade, or if you're the type who genuinely can't sit still, there's always a ranch project that needs doing.

Saturday ends the right way — barn clothes swapped for something nicer, a short walk next door to the winery, and a glass of wine at golden hour with the Hill Country laid out in front of you. You'll sleep well that night. Sunday morning you won't need to be told what to do — you'll already know. **That's the point.**

### Saturday — The Full Experience

|------|------|----------|-------------|

| 8:30–10:30 am | Hands-on | Dairy 101 — From Nan to Your Table | Strain and chill morning milk. Skim cream. Make butter the old-fashioned way — a jar, your arms, patience. Cover A2/A2 milk and taste the difference firsthand. |
| 10:30 am–Noon | Rest | Wander, Rest, or Visit Cognac | Walk out to see Cognac — patient with visitors who want to walk with him or linger for photos. Or just find shade and sit with it. |

| 1:00–3:00 pm | Rest | Afternoon Rest | Non-negotiable in July. Nap, read, be still. The farm will still be here at three. |
| 3:00–5:00 pm | Rest (opt. Hands-on) | Pool Time — or Get Your Hands Dirty | Default: the pool. You've earned every minute. Optional: tell us and we'll put you to work on a real ranch project — fence panels, T-posts, feeders, moving the meat bird tractor. |

| 7:00–7:30 pm | Rest | Clean Up & Change | Swap barn clothes for something nicer. Tonight's earned a proper evening. |
| 7:30–9:30 pm | Evening Out | Sunset Wine & Dinner at the Winery Next Door | Short walk next door. Wine at golden hour. Dinner with the Hill Country laid out in front of you. The day's work settles in here. |

## Guest Notes (Pre-Arrival Info)

- Bring closed-toe boots or sturdy shoes — flip-flops don't work near livestock
- Pack clothes you don't mind getting dirty — farm mornings are real mornings
- Bring a water bottle you'll actually use — Texas heat is non-negotiable
- Charge your camera or phone — Cognac alone is worth the memory card space
- Come with an open mind and willingness to feel uncertain — that's where the learning happens
- Nothing else — we'll take care of the rest

### Typography
- **Display / headings:** Playfair Display (Google Fonts) — weights 400, 700; also italic 400
- **Body / supporting text:** Crimson Pro (Google Fonts) — weights 300, 400; italic 300

### Activity Type Color Coding
- 🔴 Rust (`#b04a2a`) — Hands-on activity
- 🟢 Sage (`#7a8c6e`) — Rest / your time
- 🟤 Clay (`#a0683a`) — Meals & drinks

### Key Design Principles
- Warm, organic, natural aesthetic — not rustic-kitschy
- Boutique feel: generous whitespace, refined typography
- The tension between "lux" and "hard work" should come through in copy and imagery
- Timeline/schedule should feel like a narrative, not a calendar
- Mobile-responsive is essential — guests will view on phones
